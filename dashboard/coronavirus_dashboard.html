Cases, deaths and hospitalizations

HOW TO READ THE CHARTS

Please note that each chart uses two different data sets: The lines show data by the date it was reported to the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment, and the bars show data by symptom onset date or date of death.

Lines: Best for showing trend. The line charts show when cases, deaths and hospitalizations were reported to the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment. The lag time between when those occurred and when they were reported to the state can be several days. The chart uses a 7-day average to smooth out peaks and valleys in the data due to reporting lag times.

Bars: Best for showing historical data. The bar charts show when cases and hospitalizations occurred based on CDPHE's investigation into the onset date of symptoms, and when a death occurred based on the date on the death certificate. Data for most recent two weeks is not shown because it will be signifcantly revised with subsequent data releases as CDPHE investigations progress, and is not an accurate representation of recent cases, deaths and hospitalizations.

Totals: The total number of cases, deaths and hospitalizations that have been reported to the CDPHE.

Testing rate and results

HOW TO READ THE CHARTS

Bar: The bar chart shows the 7-day moving average of the number of daily tests per 100,000 people, as reported to the CDPHE. Health officials say a testing rate of 152 per 100,000 is necessary to identify the majority of people who are infected.

Line: The line chart shows a 7-day moving average of the percentage of daily tests for COVID-19 that were positive.
