Bars: Best for showing historical data older than two weeks. All data can be revised daily and data for most recent two weeks will be signifcantly revised moving forward as health department investigations progress.

Line: Best for showing trend. The lag time between when cases, deaths and hospitalizations occurred and when they were reported to the state can be several days. Using a 7-day moving average smooths out artifical peaks and valleys in the data.
